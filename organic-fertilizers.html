Nature's Nutrient Cycle

Chemical fertilizers feed the plant temporarily while starving the soil. Organic fertilizers feed the soil, which in turn builds resilient, healthy plants that can thrive for generations.

Compost
Made From:

Decomposed organic matter (leaves, food scraps, crop residue).

Benefits:

Improves soil structure, water retention, and microbial activity. Provides a slow release of broad-spectrum nutrients.

Best For: Universally beneficial; highly recommended for vegetables and foundational soil building.
Vermicompost
Made From:

Organic waste broken down by earthworms.

Benefits:

Extremely nutrient-dense. Rich in humic acids and growth hormones. Improves plant immunity.

Best For: Tomatoes, Turmeric, high-value vegetables, and seedling nurseries.
Neem Cake
Made From:

The residue left after extracting oil from neem seeds.

Benefits:

Acts as both a fertilizer and a natural pesticide. Protects roots from nematodes and soil grubs.

Best For: Chili, Onion, Tomato, and cash crops prone to root diseases.
Farm Yard Manure
Farm Yard Manure (FYM)
Made From:

Aged mixture of cow dung, urine, and agricultural litter.

Benefits:

The traditional backbone of Indian farming. Adds bulk organic matter and essential NPK (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium).

Best For: Rice, Cotton, Maize, and broad-acre staples.
Green Manure
Green Manure
Made From:

Specific cover crops (like Sunn hemp or Dhaincha) grown and then plowed back into the soil.

Benefits:

Fixes atmospheric nitrogen directly into the soil. Prevents weed growth and soil erosion during off-seasons.

Best For: Grown right before sowing Rice, Wheat, or Maize.
Bone Meal
Bone Meal
Made From:

Finely crushed animal bones.

Benefits:

Exceptional source of slow-release phosphorus and calcium. Essential for root development and flowering.

Best For: Root crops, Cotton, and heavy flowering plants.
